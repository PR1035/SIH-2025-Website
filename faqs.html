Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about rain water harvesting. Click a question to expand the answer.

Rain water harvesting is the collection and storage of rain from roofs or other surfaces to use later, either through storage tanks, recharge pits for groundwater, or direct re-use. You capture runoff, filter it, and either store it or channel it to recharge the ground.
A rooftop tank or a small storage system for household use can be installed with minimal rooftop and ground space. For groundwater recharge, small recharge pits or percolation trenches can be fitted in yards or open areas.
Yes — if you intend to use collected rainwater for drinking, it must be properly filtered and disinfected (e.g., sediment filters, activated carbon, and UV or chlorination). For non-potable uses like gardening, washing, or flushing, light filtration may be sufficient.
Yes. By capturing rooftop runoff and reducing surface runoff, rainwater harvesting reduces load on drains and local flood risk, especially during heavy storms.
