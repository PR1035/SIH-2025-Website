ADVANTAGES OF RAIN WATER HARVESTING
1) Saves Water: Collect and store rain for daily use and reduce dependence on municipal supply.
2) Reduce flooding & waterlogging: Capturing rooftop runoff prevents street floods and drain overflow.
3) Ensures water security: Provides backup supply during summers and droughts.
4) Prevents soil erosion: Slows surface runoff and protects fertile soil.
5) Cost effective: Cuts water bills and lowers need for tankers.
6) Policy alignment: Supports Jal Jeevan Mission, Atal Bhujal Yojana, and SDG-6.
7) Support sustainability: Help communities to manage water locally.
8) Recharge Groundwater: Artificial recharge pits help refill aquifers and raise water tables.
Relegan Siddhi, Maharashtra

PROBLEM: Drought prone, water scarce village with crop failure and migration.

ACTION: Community based watershed management and rainwater harvesting structures.

RESULT:

• Groundwater levels rose enabling year round farming.

• Shift from single to multi crop agriculture.

Chennai,Tamil Nadu

PROBLEM:Severe groundwater depletion and urban flooding

ACTION:In 2001,Chennai made rooftop rainwater harvesting system mandatory for all buildings.

RESULT:

• Quality of drinking water improved significantly.

• Groundwater levels rose by 50-150% in 5 years.

• Reduced tanker water dependence.

Indore, Madhya Pradesh

PROBLEM: Water Scarcity during summers and heaving rainwater runoff waste.

ACTION: Indore MC launched large scale rainwater harvesting and recharge pit campaignes.

RESULT:

• 700+ recharge structures built.

• Rainwater harvesting made city water positive.

• Contributed to Indore becoming Cleanest City.
